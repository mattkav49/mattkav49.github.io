My long-term goal is to start an agroforestry consulting business and provide environmental services to farmers and the general public. I also hope to be able to conduct further research in my field of study, such as by developing new agricultural tactics to benefit air and water quality. Additionally, I hope to continue developing new methods of using microwave remote sensing in agricultural regions such as those found in the US Corn Belt.

As manipulating and analyzing data is an important component of my work now and will continue to be so going forth, I will likely continue using R and RStudio for much of my career. Many of the packages that I have become familiar with in this class will come in handy. For instance, as I mentioned before, the tabulizer package will continue to be useful for reading tables from .pdf files and converting them to more useful data types. Additionally, the lubridate package will be very helpful for working with categorical data involving dates and times, as it has been in this class This will be very useful for tracking datasets over time, such as those involving crop progress reports. Finally, I have enjoyed learning about other tidyverse packages, such as dplyr, which has been very useful for analyzing complex and messy datasets in a variety of subject areas.
